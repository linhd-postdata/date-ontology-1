POSTDATA _Dates ontology: Description back to ToC

The dating of the works and their manifestations in the domain of poetry and in the literary domain, in general, presents some characteristics, especially in date expression.
In these domains the difficulty of dating the works following established formats must be taken into consideration. Many times dates can not be determined and are compromised by a lack of accuracy which also requires additional information elements to point at some of the issues related to the dating.

We propose the creation of a small ontology of dates in which the forms of representation of the dates are expressed in the poetic and literary context. It also provides the possibility of incorporating additional information that enrich the meaning of the expressions used for the dates, which includes both precise timestamps and approximate time intervals ((for example ussing notAfter and notBefore properties).

To treat these cases, the following characteristics are incorporated:

  1. Different ways of expressing dating ( pddate: DataExpression which is the superclass of pddate: ApproximateDateExpression, pddate: ExactDateExpression and pddate: IntervalExpression.
  2. The possibility of establishing the degree of certainty
  3. The possibility of incorporating explanatory notes about the indicated date.
