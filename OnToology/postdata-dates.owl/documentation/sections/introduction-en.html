Introduction back to ToC

In the domain of poetry and literature, it is difficult to establish dates exactly and even to find an expression something close to a date, especially in medieval works or with a certain antiquity. This problem also arises when the form of transmission or preservation does not facilitate the trace of the date of origin.

In this ontology, two classes are provided : DateEntity and DateExpression is provided.

The class pddate:DateEntity represent a temporal entity asociated to an Poetic Work, its manifestationa, event and an another entity that need a datation.
The class pdddate:DateExpression is a base of a class hierarchy. This class and its subclasses provides several modes to represent the date related with the creation or another event associated with a entity. The subclasses are: pddate:ApproximateDateExpression that permits to express a date through a sentence that indicates a date in an approximate form. pddate:ExactDateExpression is used to express a entity datation through a exact date. pddate:IntervalExpression . This class has two subclasses; pddate:ClosedIntervalExpression and pddate:OpenedIntervalExpression. All of them can used to express an date through a range of dates.
